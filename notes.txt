Epoch: One full pass over your entire dataset. If your text file has 10,000 sentences, one epoch means the model has looked at all 10,000 sentences exactly once.
Why 1-3? Because small datasets overfit rapidly. If you run 50 epochs on a 5MB text file, the model will just memorize it word-for-word instead of learning the style.

Batch Size: Think of this as pagination or chunking. You can't load a 20MB text file and all the model's math into your RAM/VRAM at once—it will crash. 
So, you process the data in "batches" (e.g., 4 or 8 sequences at a time). The model reads a batch, calculates its error, updates its internal weights, and then grabs the next batch.
